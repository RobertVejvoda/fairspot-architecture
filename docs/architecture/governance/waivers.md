# Waivers

Waivers track accepted exceptions to architecture principles, standards, or controls.

Waivers are not a way to hide gaps. They are explicit risk acceptances with an owner and review trigger. If an exception becomes the new normal, update the principle/standard through change control instead of keeping a permanent waiver.

## Rules

- Waivers must have an owner and review date.
- Security/privacy waivers require explicit risk acceptance.
- Permanent waivers should be converted into a decision or resolved by changing the standard.
- Waivers that affect a customer-facing deployment must state whether they block pilot use, require customer disclosure, or are accepted for demo only.
